Football, also known as soccer in some countries, is the most popular sport in the world, played and watched by millions across every continent. It is governed globally by FIFA (Fédération Internationale de Football Association), and its most prestigious tournament, the FIFA World Cup, is held every four years and draws in billions of viewers. The game is played between two teams of 11 players each, and the objective is to score goals by getting the ball into the opposing team’s net. Football is not only a sport but also a cultural phenomenon, uniting people across races, religions, and borders.

The sport’s origin dates back to ancient civilizations, but modern football began in 19th-century England with the establishment of rules by the Football Association in 1863. Today, professional football leagues exist in nearly every country, with elite clubs like Real Madrid, Manchester United, and Barcelona enjoying global fan bases. Great players like Pelé, Diego Maradona, Lionel Messi, and Cristiano Ronaldo have left a lasting impact on the game. Football also plays a significant role in economic and social development, offering opportunities, inspiring youth, and even influencing politics in various regions.
