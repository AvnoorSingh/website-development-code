Facts About Airplane

Airplanes are one of the most significant inventions of the 20th century, revolutionizing transportation by enabling fast, long-distance travel across continents and oceans. The Wright brothers, Orville and Wilbur, flew the first powered airplane in 1903 in North Carolina, USA. Since then, aviation technology has advanced rapidly, with modern commercial jets capable of flying at altitudes above 35,000 feet and speeds over 900 km/h. Airplanes rely on the principles of aerodynamics, especially lift, thrust, drag, and weight, to stay in the air and navigate through various weather conditions.

Planes serve multiple purposes beyond passenger transport. Cargo aircraft deliver goods across the globe, while military planes are used for defense, surveillance, and strategic missions. Special aircraft also help in firefighting, medical emergencies, and scientific research. Aircraft are designed with high safety standards and undergo regular maintenance to ensure passenger and crew safety. The aviation industry continues to evolve with efforts toward sustainable flying, including the development of electric planes and the use of alternative fuels to reduce carbon emissions.
